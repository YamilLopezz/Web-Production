What is a multiverse?



A Multiverse is hypothetical group of multiple universes out there. The universe is ever expanding hence, there has been a hypotheses that we are not the only universe out there and rather there are ore than one and we simply live in one of the many. Together the universes comprise of everything that exists whether it'd be space, time, dark matter, energy, and the physics that are used to describe such hypothetical scenarios. It all first began in Dublin in 1952, a researcher named Erwin Schrodinger gave a lecture -- a lecture which in the end where its listeners deemed him an absolute lunatic when he gave his then nonsensical and other worldly hypothetical scenario. He proposed that his equations seemed to describe several different histories, "these were not alternatives, but all really happen simultaneously." The term multiple universes was coined "multiverse" in 1895 by American Philosopher and Psychologist, William James.

Evidence on the Multiverse?



In 2010, scientists, one of them being Stephen M. Feeney analyzed data from a probe. The probe was called WMAP - Wilkinson Microwave Anisotrophy Probe. They claimed to find evidence that our universe collided with other universes once ago long in the past. We called this type of collision a bubble universe collision. But the evidence came back null as there was no type of significant evidence such as gravitational pull of space time fabric to prove that the bubble universe collision indeed had occured. NASA has stepped up to the topic of the Universe once before. In the early 1990's, NASA was fairly certain about the expansion of the universe although not the multiverse. Space has enough dark energy and dark matter to continuously expand and recollapse. In 1998, the Hubble Space Telescope observed a distance supernovae that gave evidence that the universe has actually been accelerating more than they thought. Even to this day, we are still unsure of how to explain this expansion or what is causing it.
